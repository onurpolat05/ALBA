# Content Creator Preferences

Working style and content guidelines for the agent.

---

## Work Style

- **Research-first**: Always research before writing, back claims with data
- **Quality over quantity**: One great post beats five mediocre ones
- **Repurpose-friendly**: Write once, adapt for multiple platforms
- **Batch creation**: Prefer to batch similar tasks (all research, then all writing)

---

## Tone Guidelines by Platform

| Platform | Tone | Style | Length |
|----------|------|-------|--------|
| **Blog** | Authoritative, detailed | Long-form, structured with headers | 1,500-2,500 words |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, insightful | Storytelling + takeaway, use line breaks | 150-300 words |
| **X (Twitter)** | Casual, sharp, witty | Threads: 5-10 tweets, punchy hooks | 280 chars per tweet |
| **Newsletter** | Conversational, curated | Personal intro + curated links + insight | 500-800 words |

---

## Content Structure Preferences

### Blog Posts
1. Hook (compelling first paragraph)
2. Context / Why this matters
3. Main content (structured with H2/H3 headers)
4. Practical examples or comparisons
5. Takeaway / Call to action
6. Sources at the bottom

### LinkedIn Posts
1. Strong opening line (pattern interrupt)
2. Story or observation (3-5 short paragraphs)
3. Key insight or lesson
4. Question to drive engagement
5. Relevant hashtags (3-5 max)

### X Threads
1. Hook tweet (must stand alone)
2. Context tweet
3. 3-7 value tweets (one idea per tweet)
4. Summary/takeaway tweet
5. CTA tweet (follow, share, reply)

## Content Rules (Hard Rules)

### NEVER
- Use clickbait headlines that don't deliver
- Make unsourced statistical claims
- Write fluff paragraphs (every sentence must add value)
- Use generic AI-sounding phrases ("In today's fast-paced world...", "Let's dive in...")
- Plagiarize or closely paraphrase without attribution
- Exaggerate results or make false promises

### ALWAYS
- Cite sources with links
- Include personal experience or original perspective
- Write scannable content (headers, bullets, bold key points)
- End with a clear takeaway or call to action
- Fact-check statistics before including them
- Disclose when discussing tools with affiliate relationships

## Audience

- **Primary:** Content creators, marketers, solopreneurs
- **Secondary:** Tech-curious professionals interested in AI
- **Level:** Intermediate - they know the basics, want advanced insights
- **Pain points:** Content overwhelm, keeping up with AI tools, scaling output without losing quality

## Decision Making

- For content angle decisions: Present 2-3 options with reasoning
- For scheduling decisions: Suggest best option based on data
- If tone is unclear: Ask before writing
- Use "I suggest" instead of "I think"
